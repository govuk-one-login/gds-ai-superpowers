# WCAG 2.4.11 Focus Not Obscured (Minimum) (AA, new in 2.2) — deep

> Pending the programme accessibility owner's review (like the WCAG spine).
> Technique and testing guidance — **not** a restatement of the normative SC text.

Pull when 2.4.11 applicability is contested or unclear (new in WCAG 2.2; commonly missed).

**How to test (technique)**
- Tab through the page; at each focus stop confirm the focused component is **not entirely
  hidden** by author-created overlays (sticky headers/footers, cookie bars, chat widgets).
  Partial obscuring passes the Minimum (AA); full obscuring fails.
- Test at small viewports and with zoom, where sticky elements overlap more.
- Watch the common trap: a sticky header that covers a field as you tab up to it.

**Pitfalls**
- A sticky cookie/consent banner covering the focused control beneath it.
- `scroll-margin`/anchor offsets not accounting for a fixed header, so focus lands under it.
- A floating action/chat button overlapping focused content in the corner.

**Example / anti-example**
- *Bad:* tabbing to a field scrolls it under a fixed header; the focus ring is fully hidden.
- *Good:* `scroll-padding-top` reserves the header height so focused items sit below it, visible.
